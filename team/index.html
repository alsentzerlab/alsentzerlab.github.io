Emily Alsentzer, PhD is an Assistant Professor of Biomedical Data Science and, by courtesy, of Computer Science at Stanford University. Previously, she was a postdoctoral fellow at Brigham and Women's Hospital and Harvard Medical School (HMS) where she worked to deploy ML models within the Mass General Brigham healthcare system. She received her PhD from the Health Science & Technology (HST) program at MIT & HMS, co-advised by Zak Kohane and Pete Szolovits.

Fiona is a second-year PhD student in the Department of Biomedical Data Science. Her research focuses on knowledge-grounded language model systems for biomedical reasoning, with a focus on rare disease diagnosis. She is also interested in multimodal retrieval and reasoning, particularly for biomedical visual question answering. She previously received her B.S. and M.Eng. in Computer Science from MIT.
